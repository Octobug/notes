动名词的构成即是在动词后面加 `-ing`，构成 doing 的形式。
它在意义上相当于一个名词，但同时又保留动词的特征。
说它相当于一个名词，是因为它在句中主要充当名词可以充当的成分，比如**主语、宾语、表语或定语**。
说它保留动词的特征，是因为它可以**有自己的宾语，有自己的时态和语态变化，还可保留自己的逻辑主语**。

## 1. 动名词的作用（一）：作主语

与不定式作主语不同，动名词作主语一般直接放在句首，谓语动词用**单数**。
只有在某些特定句型中才会把动名词放在句末作主语，句首用 it 形式主语来指代，类似于不定式作主语的结构。

### 1.1 动名词位于句首作主语

动名词作主语一般直接放在句首，谓语动词用单数。

`1` Hating people is like burning down your own house to get rid of a rat.

- 句子主语是 hating people 这个动名词短语，而不是 people。
- `译` 憎恨别人，就好像为了赶走一只老鼠而把自己的房子烧掉。

`2` Getting mad at others means other people are getting control of your
emotions!

- `译` 生别人的气就意味着让别人控制了你的情绪！

`3` Using the mass media to point the finger at China will not help get the
global deal we need to avert climate change disaster. Not only have rich
countries historically caused the problem, but they are also importing emissions
from the developing world.

- 利用大众传媒对中国横加指责，无助于达成我们制止气候变化灾难所需的全球协议。
  富国不仅在历史上是这一问题的始作俑者，它们现在也正在从发展中国家进口排放权。

`4`

- A: I studied French in high school but I never really learned it until I spent
  the summer in Paris.
- B: Really using a language makes all the difference, doesn't it?
  - 句子主语是 really using a language 这个动名词短语，using 前面有副词 really 修饰。
  - `译` A: 我是在高中时学习法语的，但直到上次去巴黎过了一个夏天我才觉得我真的学会了。
    B: 真正去使用一门外语感觉完全不一样，对吧？

`5` Being deeply loved by someone gives you strength; while loving someone
deeply gives you courage.

- `译` 被爱给你力量；爱人给你勇气。

`6` No man is an isolated island. Your circle of friends is there to give you
moral support. Spending time and engaging in worthwhile activities with them
could give you a very satisfying feeling. Nothing feels better than having group
support.

- `译` 没有人是一座孤岛，因为你有自己的朋友圈子，这会给你精神上的支持。
  和朋友待在一起，参加一些有意义的活动，这可以给你一种非常满足的感觉。
  没有什么比能获得团体的支持让你感觉更好的了。

`7` ____ by transferring the blame to others is often called scapegoating.

- A. The eliminated problems
- B. Eliminating problems
- C. Eliminate problems
- D. Problems are eliminated
- `答案` B。句子缺主语，动名词短语作句子主语。
- `译` 通过把罪责推卸到别人身上，以此来逃避问题，这叫做找替罪羊。

`8` ____ to inanimate objects, such as machines, is a form of animism.

- A. When attributing emotion
- B. Attributing emotion
- C. Emotion is attributed
- D. If emotion is attributed
- `答案` B。句子缺主语，动名词短语作为句子主语。
- `译` 认为没有生命的物体（如机器）也具有感情，这是一种“万物有灵论”的观点。

### 1.2 动名词谓语句末作主语

把动名词放在句末作主语一般仅限于某些特定句型。

#### 一、it is no good/no use doing sth

表示“干某事没有用”。这里的 it 是形式主语，真正的主语是句末的动名词短语 doing sth.。
其中，no good 可以替换为 any/some good, any/some/no use 以及 a waste of time 等。

`1` It's no use crying over spilt milk.

- `=` Crying over spilt milk is no use.
- `译` 后悔没有用。

`2` It's no use talking to that parrot.

- `译` 跟那个鹦鹉说话没用。

`3` Is it any good trying to explain?

- `译` 努力解释有用吗？

`4` It's not much use my buying salmon if you don't like fish.

- `译` 如果你不喜欢吃鱼的话，那么我就没必要买鲑鱼了。

`5` It's simply a waste of time and money seeing that movie.

- `译` 看那部电影纯粹是浪费时间和金钱。

`7` It's a waste of time trying to explain to you.

- `译` 跟你解释那件事情简直就是浪费时间。

#### 二、there is no point/use/good (in) doing sth

表示“干某事没有必要/意义”，“干某事没用”。

`8` There is no point in my going out to date someone. I might really like if I
met him at the time, but who, right now, has no chance of being anything to me
but a transitional man.

- `译` 现在让我出去和人约会没有用。如果我碰上了意中人，我自然会爱上他的。可是在现在这个时候我跟谁也不会有结果的，除非是为了解解闷。

`9` There is no use your arguing with him.

- `译` 你没必要和他争论。

`11` There's little use in hurrying to get there.

- `译` 没有必要这么匆匆忙忙地赶过去。

#### 三、there is no doing sth

相当于 it's impossible to do sth. 或 we can't do sth.，表示“不可能做某事”。

`12` There is no telling what will happen tomorrow.

- `译` 谁也无法预知明天会发生什么。

`13` There's no knowing the future.

- `=` It's impossible to know the future.
- `=` We can't know the future.
- `译` 谁也无法预知未来。

此外还有：

- 毋庸置疑
  - there is no denying the fact that...
  - there is no gainsaying the fact that...

## 2. 动名词的作用（二）：作宾语

动名词主要就是放在某些特定的动词或者介词后面作宾语，此外还可以用在某些特定句型结构中。

### 2.1 在动词后面作宾语

在这些动词（短语）后面一般要用动名词作宾语：

- admit, advise, anticipate, appreciate, avoid
- consider, contemplate
- delay, deny, detest, discuss, dislike
- encourage, endure, enjoy, escape, excuse
- fancy, favor, feel like, finish, forbid, forgive
- give up
- can't help
- imagine, involve
- leave off
- mention, mind, miss
- overlook
- permit, postpone, practice, prohibit, put off
- resent, resist, risk
- can't stand, suggest
- tolerate
- understand

`1` Many of the things we do involve taking some risk in order to achieve a
satisfactory result.

- `译` 要想取得满意的结果，我们必须冒一些风险。

`2` Putting in a new window will involve cutting away part of the roof.

- `译` 要想安装一扇新窗户就必须把房顶打掉一块。

`3` Being a bad-tempered man, he would not tolerate having his lectures
interrupted.

`4` Peter, who had been driving all day, suggested stopping at the next town.

`5` They are considering buying the house before the prices go up.

`6` She was so angry that she felt like throwing something at him.

`7` I can't imagine saying goodbye to the horses. It's become my way of life for
the last three years.

- `译` 我无法想象跟马告别。在过去三年里，这已经成为我的生活方式。

`9` I will overlook your being so rude to my sister this time but don't let it
happen again.

- overlook 后面接了物主代词 your，再接动名词 being，两者一起构成动名词的复合结构 your
  being...，其中 your 是动名词 being 的逻辑主语。
- `译` 这次你对我妹妹态度粗鲁就算了，但下不为例。

### 2.2 在介词后面作宾语

介词后面不能直接用动词原形作宾语，必须改为动名词。
不过，由于 to 可以是不定式的小品词，所以后面可以接动词原形。
因此在遇到 to 时，必须分清它是作为介词还是作为不定式的符号。

介词 to 短语后面必须接动名词，常见的有：

- amount to doing
- be accustomed to do/doing
- be averse to doing
- be/get used to doing
- be devoted to doing
- be dedicated to doing
- be opposed to doing
- dedicate...to doing
- devote...to doing
- get round to doing
- in addition to doing
- look forword to doing
- object to doing
- prefer doing to doing
- take to doing
- the alternative/approach/solution to doing

`1` I am looking forword to hearing from you.

`2` I am used to sleeping with the lights on.

`3`

- I am accustomed to sleeping with the lights on.
- I am accustomed to sleep with the lights on.

`4` The students object to increasing their tuition.

- `译` 学生反对提高学费。

`5` After a long delay I finally got round to making inquiries into the matter.

- `译` 拖了很长时间以后，我才终于抽出时间来过问这件事。

`6` As an alternative to using a single-occupancy vehicle, carpooling involves
two to five commuters sharing a vehicle.

- an alternative to 里的 to 是介词，后面接动名词 using。
  另外，involve 后面接了动名词的复合结构 two to five commuters sharing a vehicle，其中
  two to five commuters 充当动名词 sharing a vehicle 的逻辑主语。
- `译` 作为单人驾车之外的选择，拼车是指两到五个通勤者共乘一辆车。

### 2.3 在特定的句型中作宾语

英语中有很多固定的句型结构要求接动名词作宾语。

#### 一、“做某事遇到了麻烦/困难”

最常见的是表示“做某事遇到了麻烦/困难”，或“做某事很开心”的句型结构。

have +

- a difficult time
- a good time
- a hard time
- difficulty
- fun
- pleasure
- problems
- trouble

(in) doing sth.

`1`

- A: How do you find your new apartment?
- B: Well, it's quite nice really, although I have a hard time getting used to
  living in a big place.
  - have a hard time 后面接了动名词 getting。另外 get used to 后面也要接动名词。
  - `译`
    - A: 你觉得你的新公寓怎么样？
    - B: 哦，真的很好，尽管我一时还没有适应住在这么大的房子。

`2`

- A: We are having all kinds of trouble finding a new apartment for next year.
  What about you?
- B: My roommate and I decided that this year we'd stay put.

`3`

- A: You certainly have a lot of clocks. There seems to be one in every room.
- B: My family gave them to me because I have trouble keeping track of the time.

`4`

- A: I had a hard time getting through this novel.
- B: I know how you feel. Who can remember the names of 35 different characters.

`5` I'm having a hard time keeping up in my biology class.

`6` Native speakers of European languages often have a very difficult time in
learning one of the Asian languages.

`7` When we moved to Canada a few years ago, we had a hard time communicating
with Canadians, so we started to read a lot of Endlish books and magazines to
improve our conversation and listening skills.

`8` They turn the volume up full blast and begin to party. The music is so loud
that we have a hard time carrying on a conversation without raising our voices.

`9` I have learned, however, that change always seems more difficult at the
outside, and if I work hard at remaining positive and view changes as
opportunities to grow, I have an easier time dealing with them.

- `译` 不过，我已经明白，变化一开始似乎总是更困难。但是，如果我努力保持乐观积极的心态，并且把变化当作是成长的机会，我就会比较容易应对变化。

⚠️ 下列短语的后面要接不定式：

- have (no) time to do sth.
- take the trouble to do sth.
- trouble to do sth.

`10` I worked so late in the office last night that I hardly had time to catch
the last bus.

#### 二、表示“情不自禁”“不得不”的短语

这些短语包括：

- can't help doing
- can't hold back from doing
- can't keep back from doing
- can't keep from doing
- can't resist doing

`11` No one can help liking Tom; he is such a cute boy.

- `译` 没有人不喜欢 Tom，他实在太可爱了。

⚠️ 下列短语要接动词原形：

- can't but do
- can't choose but do
- can't help but do

#### 三、其他句型结构

还有其他一些短语，比如 be worth doing, be busy doing。这类短语用主动形式表示被动的意思。
be worth doing 可以转为其他句型结构：be worth of being done/to be done。

`12` The book is worth reading.

- The book is worthy of being read.
- The book is worthy to be read.

`13` The money will be used to keep a space vehicle on the moon busy sending
back to the earth photographs and other information about the surface of the
moon.

- `译` 这些钱将被用来使月球上的航空飞行器持续不停地把有关月球表面的图片和其他信息发回到地球。

## 3. 动名词的作用（三）：作表语

动名词用作表语，主要是直接放在 be 动词后面，以补充说明句子主语的具体内容。

`1` His hobby is collecting stamps.

`2` Reading is permitting a man to talk a long time, and refusing you the right
to answer.

- `译` 阅读，就如同允许作者一个人长时间地说，而你却无权回应。

## 4. 动名词的作用（四）：作定语

动名词用作定语，主要是放在被修饰的名词前面，一般表示所修饰名词的用途。

`1` a sleeping car: 卧铺车厢

`2` a reading room: 阅览室

`3` a swimming pool: 游泳池

在英语中，动名词作定语的情况不多，远没有**现在分词**作定语常见。

## 5. 动名词的逻辑主语

和不定式类似，动名词也是动词的一种变化形式。既然是动词，就应该有动作的执行者，即主语。
句子的主语和动名词的动作执行者并不总是统一的，所以称动名词的主语为逻辑主语，以区分句子的主语。
动名词与其逻辑主语一起构成动名词的复合结构。

### 5.1 动名词复合结构的构成

物主代词（如 his, my, your 等）或所有格名词（如 Mary's）与动名词连用，即构成动名词的复合结构，用来引出动名词的逻辑主语。
当句子的主语并不是动名词动作的执行者时，往往需要给出动名词的动作发出者，也就是动名词的逻辑主语。

`1`

- Clint insisted on reading the letter.
  - 句子的主语 Clint 同时充当动名词 reading 的逻辑主语。
  - `译` Clint 坚持要看信。
- Clint insisted on my reading the letter.
  - 句子的主语 Clint 并非动名词 reading 的逻辑主语，“我”才是 reading 的逻辑主语。
  - `译` Clint 坚持要我看信。

`2`

- I object to making private calls on this phone.
  - 逻辑主语不明确。
  - `译` 我不赞成用这部电话打私人电话。
- I object to his making private calls on this phone.
  - `译` 我不赞成他用这部电话打私人电话。

`3`

- Would you mind telling us the whole story?
  - `译` 你是否愿意把事情的经过告诉我们？
- Would you mind Tom's telling us the whole story?
  - `=` Would you mind if Tom tells us the whole story?
  - `译` 你是否愿意让 Tom 把事情的经过告诉我们？

`4`

- He disliked working late.
  - `译` 他不喜欢加班。
- He disliked his wife's working late.
  - `译` 他不喜欢他妻子加班。

`5` The eye of an animal is quite easy to see. How then can the eyes be
prevented from spoiling the animal's blending with its environment? One solution
would be for the animal to keep its eyes closed when danger threatens.

- `译` 动物的眼睛很容易被发现，因此，如何防止眼睛不破坏动物与其周围环境的融合呢？
  有一个办法是，当危险来临时，动物可以把眼睛闭上。

💡 在某些情况下，动名词的逻辑主语可以不是物主代词或名词所有格，而是直接采用宾格代词或普通格名词。

`6` That yearning for study was still there. I cam here with no thought of it
being an unusual thing at all.

- 这里的动名词复合结构是 it being，由宾格代词 it 与动名词 being 构成。这里也可以改为 its
  being。
- `译` 我仍有强烈的学习的愿望，到这来我一点也不觉得有什么反常的。

`7` Romantic love is very much a part of American culture. Movies, TV shows and
books in America all picture people falling in love. Americans know no romance
is perfect, but still they try to find the ideal person.

- 动名词复合结构 people falling in love 由普通格名词 people 与动名词 falling 构成。
  这里也可以说成 people's falling in love...
- `译` 浪漫爱情是美国文化的重要组成部分。美国的电影、电视和书籍都描绘人们如何坠入爱河。美国人知道没有哪段罗曼史是完美的，但他们还是努力去寻找自己理想的意中人。

`8` Physically and mentally women are by far the superior to men. The old
chestnut about women being more emotional than men has been forever destroyed by
the facts of two great wars.

- women being more emotional than men 也可以改为 women's being more emotional
  than men
- `译` 女性在身体上和精神上都大大优越于男性。两次世界大战的事实永远粉碎了女人比男人更多愁善感的陈词滥调。

### 5.2 所有格与普通格的使用规律

#### 一、若动名词的复合结构在句中作主语，最好用所有格形式

`1` Tom's refusing to accept the invitation upset me.

- Tom's refusing to accept the invitation 在句中作主语，因为不宜用普通格复合结构
  Tom refusing...
- `译` Tom 拒绝接受邀请，这让我很恼火。

`2` His refusing to accept the invitation upset me.

#### 二、动名词的复合结构在句中作宾语时，用普通格或所有格均可

`3` Do you mind me making a suggestion?

- `=` Do you mind **my** making a suggestion?
- `译` 我提个建议，你介意吗？

`4` I am annoyed about John forgetting to pay.

- `=` I am annoyed about John's forgetting to pay.
- `译` John 忘记付钱，这让我很生气。

`5` I remember him once offering to help us if we ever got into trouble.

- `译` 我记得他曾经承诺，一旦我们遇到麻烦他就会来帮我们。

`6` The latest revolution isn't simply a matter of gentlemen reading other
gentlemen's e-mail.

- 也可以用 gentlemen's reading other gentlemen's e-mail
- `译` 最近的这次革命不只是一些人偷看另一些人的电子邮件的问题。

`7` All these conditions tend to increase the probability of a child committing
a criminal act.

- 也可以用 a child's committing a criminal act.
- `译` 所有这些条件往往会增加青少年犯罪的可能性。

`8` With other audiences you mustn't attempt to cut in with humor as they will
resent an outsider making disparaging remarks about their canteen or their
chairman.

- resent 后面要求接动名词作宾语，这里用了普通格名词的动名词复合结构，也可以用名词所有格
  an outsider's。
- `译` 对于其他听众，你就不能试图插入这种幽默，因为他们不喜欢外人对他们的餐厅或老总有如此微词。

#### 三、当动名词的逻辑主语是无生命的“物”而不是“人”时，最好用普通格

`9` The noise of the desks being opened and closed can be heard out in the
street.

- the desks 是“物”，所以用普通格。
- `译` 课桌开关的声音在大街上都能听得到。

`10` There is a necessity for this type of houses being built.

- `译` 有必要修建这种类型的房屋。

`11` In the event of the project not being a success, the investors stand to
lose up to $30 million.

- `译` 万一这个项目投资失败，投资者将会损失 3,000 万美元。

`12` The young leading the young, is like the blind leading the blind; "They
will both fall into the ditch." The only sure guide is he who has often gone the
road which you want to go. Let me be that guide, who have gone all roads, and
who can consequently point out to you the best.

- 这里 the young 虽然是表示“年轻人”，但由于是形容词，形容词没有所有格形式，所以不说
  the young's。
- `译` 青年为青年引路，如同盲人为盲人引路；“两人会一同掉进沟里。”唯一可靠的向导是这样的人：他常走的路正是你想走的路。
  且让我当这样一位向导吧，我走过各种各样的路，所以能够为你指明门路。

#### 四、当动名词的逻辑主语是不定代词时，最好用普通格

如：

- anyone
- everyone
- someone

`13` He was awakened by someone knocking on the door.

- `译` 有人敲门，把他吵醒了。

#### 五、当动名词的逻辑主语由较长的一组词构成时，最好用普通格

`14` There is no point any one of us arguing with him.

- any one of us 是一个短语，不便使用所有格代词。
- `译` 我们谁与他争论都没有用。

`15` I've always had in my mind a dream of my father, mother and I living
together.

- 动名词逻辑主语 my father, mother and I 是一个名词短语，不宜使用所有格
- `译` 我一直梦想和父母生活在一起。

`16` Do you remember John and his father coming to see us last Christmas?

- 动名词逻辑主语 John and his father 是一个名词短语，不宜使用所有格
- `译` 你还记得 John 和他父亲去年圣诞节来看望我们吗？

`17` Can you imagine a European television station putting a Pakistani immigrant
family on television and asking if they'd learned to use a knife and fork yet?

- `译` 你能想象一个欧洲的电视台采访一个巴基斯坦移民家庭，然后问他们“你们学会使用刀叉了吗？”这类问题吗？

`18` As an alternative to using a single-occupancy vehicle, carpooling involves
two to five commuters sharing a vehicle.

- `译` 作为单人驾车之外的选择，拼车是指两到五个通勤者共乘一辆车。
